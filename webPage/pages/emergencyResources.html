Emergency Resources

Louisiana State Emergency Resources

  • Louisiana Department of Education – Flooding Support
    The Louisiana Department of Education provides direct support for students, families, and school systems impacted by flooding. Their hotline and support email help connect families with emergency assistance, resource information, and recovery guidance.

    Flooding Hotline: 1-844-348-4082
    Email: LA_Flooding_Help@ed.gov

    Disaster Distress Helpline
    24/7 crisis counseling for anyone affected by disasters.
    Phone: 1-800-985-5990
    Text: “TalkWithUs” to 66746
    TTY (Hearing Impaired): 1-800-846-8517
  • Louisiana Emergency Preparedness Guide (PDF)
    A statewide guide covering evacuation planning, storm readiness, emergency kits, family communication plans, and flood safety procedures.
  • Louisiana Flood Recovery Resources
    FEMA registration guidance, rebuilding assistance, insurance support, recovery programs, and official state resources for flood-impacted residents.
  • Louisiana Department of Health — After Flooding
    Public health guidance for mold cleanup, contaminated water, food safety, sanitation, and home recovery following floods.

New Orleans Emergency Information

  • NOLA Emergency Resource Centers
    Locations of shelters, cooling centers, charging stations, and community support hubs during storms or extended outages.
  • NOLA Flooding Preparedness
    Flood zone maps, sandbag pickup sites, real-time water level monitoring, and preparedness guidance for New Orleans neighborhoods.
  • NOLA Hurricane Preparedness
    Evacuation routes, shelter information, storm categories, and readiness steps tailored specifically for the New Orleans region.

Grand Isle & Port Fourchon Resources

  • Port Fourchon Weather & Storm Info
    Live marine conditions, storm alerts, and coastal impact tracking for one of Louisiana’s most vulnerable industrial ports.
  • Grand Isle Flood Fact Finder & Dashboard
    Real-time flood alerts, tide data, erosion changes, and interactive historical mapping of storms impacting Grand Isle.

Louisiana Coastal Erosion Plan

  • 2023 Coastal Master Plan
    Louisiana’s 50-year plan to protect and restore the coast. The 2023 plan outlines major strategies including barrier island restoration, marsh creation, levee improvements, sediment diversions, and investments to reduce flood risk for vulnerable communities. It provides scientific modeling and project priorities for managing long-term land loss and increasing coastal resilience.
  • 2026 Fiscal Plan (Funding for Coastal Master Plan)
    The annual fiscal plan that funds projects identified in the Coastal Master Plan. It details how Louisiana allocates money from federal grants, offshore energy revenue, state programs, and hazard mitigation funds to support restoration, flood protection, and climate adaptation projects statewide.
